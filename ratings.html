Hierarchial Sunburst & Scattergram

(with year-genre hierarchy, size represents imdb score and color scale is build on gross values for SF movies dataset) & (imsb score and gross movie ratio for all movies in movie_metadata dataset)



Question

Where do movies filmed in San Francisco rate within the overall imdb movie ratings and gross? Has it changed over the years?

Answer

The answer to the question is easier to get using interactivity. For now we cannot see any particular patterns, though overall we can see that a lot of movies got high imdb score and reasonable gross.

Encoding & Wrangling

Sunburst elements include hierarchy of year by genre. The size of the cell is encoded by the total sum of imdb score of movies in this hierarchy cell, so the higher the cumulative imdb score in a year, the larger the cell. The color scheme shows the gross, the darker color shows the higher gross.
Scattergram shows imdb scores on y axis and and gross on y axis. Each movie is encoded by circle and places on (x,y) where x is it's imdb score and y is it's gross.

Additional Wrangling

There are less movies than on the map,because the movies from SF movies were merged with IMDB 5000 Movie Dataset to get ratings data. The second dataset doesn't include movies released after 2016 and doesn't include any televison series, which resulted in reducing SF movie elements in the ratings diagrams. After first merging all rows were merged again by same title-director-year, to remove duplicate data in ratings. It was used in location map: if a movie was filmed in different locations in SF, it had more than one row.

Interactivity

The sunburst chart will have a zoom on click implementation. After clicking in a particular year region, we will see all movies filmed this year by titles. After hovering over the region, we will see all movies from this region highlighted on the scattergram (see img. below). After hovering over the movie title after zooming, we will see this particular movie highlighted on the scattergram (see img. below).
